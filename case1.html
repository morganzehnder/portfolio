Invision Prototype

The short version.

There are various tools on the web for saving content. Users find themselves saving and sorting content in various ways dependent on the nature of the content and functions of specific tools. Dockit is a platform built for individuals and teams to store and organize all types of web content, with the ability to create or upload supplemental content.

Users need a single platform fit to store all types of content for ease of organization and file relocation.

Design a platform fit to store and showcase all types of content so users have all of their files in a single location.

User Survey

Conducted to understand how users currently store content, what methods they use to save content, what types of content they save the most, and what is missing from their current file storage system.

Results

  • 100% store content on their smartphones, 83% on laptops, 37% on tablets
  • 66% bookmark content on their browser bar, 53% download it, and 26% use a web app
  • 81% use their browser, 62% use Pinterest, 12.5% use Pocket, and 50% use Facebook
  • 88% of users work in a collaborative environment and 75% share files often with their team

Conclusions

  • Build a fully responsive web application that will work flawlessly on phones and tablets
  • Develop a browser application so users can save content with one-click
  • Understand likes/dislikes of closest competitors Pinterest and Pocket
  • Include collaborative and sharing capabilities

Competitive Analysis

File storage web applications offer different sets of tools for perfoming relatively the same task - saving content. Before deciding on the features to include in Dockit, I conducted a competitive analysis to assess the strengths and weaknesses of competitors against our users needs.

Pinterest

Quick and easy for saving visual content

Not good for any non-visual content

Pocket

Ability to tag right when you save, easy organization

Cannot upload or create supplemental content

Dropbox

Easy to collaborate and share files

No way to save files directly from web

Evernote

Ability to clip content, don't have to save whole web page

More complicated interface and organizational system, focus on text-based, educational material

Styleguide

For Dockit I kept style choices simple and engaging. Users spend a lot of time surfing the web and Dockit is a tool that would get frequent use. Understanding that, I kept the interface friendly and uncomplicated with warm color choices paired with grounding, darker teal hues.

I selected one font for this project, Lato. Lato is an elegant, sleek sans-serif font that reads easily in body text and has some surprising forms when enlarged for header texts. Hence, it allowed me to stick with a single font for this project. Again, keeping things simple and clean for users.

Name

Below you can see why exactly I settled on the name Dockit. Originally I racked my brain for words related to files, web files, ideas, inspiration, content, etc. I landed on the word "docket" because of it's literal relationship to "file". My thought process took off from there.

Logo Design

With the function of the Dockit revolving around storing files, the immediate jumping off point for me in terms of logo design - a plane. A flat piece of paper, an old school representation of a “file”. I played around with color, arrangement, and movement. All with planes as the foundation.

The use of abstract shapes with a hint of a retro vibe did not stop at the logo, but also carried through with the custom Dockit illustrations shown following.

What I liked

What I didn't

Play with planes, abstract representation of files. Look of “portal”, so to speak.

Logo and text didn’t seem to “fit” together. Appeared disjointed.

One plane appears to be “docked” on another.

Text didn’t ever fit the way I wanted it to across the two planes.

Movement of graphic, several planes “files” moving backwards.

Graphic didn’t look as clean from further away. Had to make text stand out at expense of making graphic harder to view/make sense of.

Final Logo

The breaking up of “dockit” clearly indicates how the name is meant to be pronounced and what it implies. This version is clean, easy to read and representative of brand. It continues to play on planes moving backwards and physically “docks” the text on planes.

Custom Illustrations

Cohesiveness and design patterns are a huge part of branding. I design and created these illustrations to further bring my design together. They also serve a practical purpose, abstractly showing the functions of Dockit through the use of simple, colorful shapes.

The two-toned icons representing the types of files Dockit is built to store are carried through the remainder of the icons, depicting the basic functionality of the tool.

5 Second Test

I conducted a five second test of my homepage to ensure my landing conveys it’s brand and message quickly to users.

  • 80% of testers believed the page looked trustworthy
  • Almost every user noticed a different item on the homepage
  • 90% of users understood the nature of the site

Takeaway: Decide what is most important on the homepage, simplify, and make it stand out
